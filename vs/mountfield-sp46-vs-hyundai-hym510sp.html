Verdict — which one to buy

Our take

UK budget self-propelled rivals. The Hyundai cuts wider (51cm vs 46cm) and has a steel deck; the Mountfield has the better engine and stronger used-market supply. Hyundai for new-buying value, Mountfield for used-buying confidence.

Pros & cons of each

Mountfield SP46

Pros

  • Best-value self-propelled petrol on the UK market
  • Stiga engine is reliable and parts are everywhere
  • Light at 27kg — easy to push and store
  • Huge used supply keeps prices low

Cons

  • Plastic deck not as durable as steel
  • Cable-operated drive less smooth than Honda
  • Rear flap hinge a known weak point
Hyundai HYM510SP

Pros

  • 51cm cut is wide for the price
  • Steel deck unusual at this price point
  • Self-propelled with electric start option
  • Hyundai 3-year warranty new

Cons

  • Hyundai branded but engine is generic
  • Heavier than rivals
  • Parts can be slow to source
