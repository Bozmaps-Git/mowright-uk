Verdict — which one to buy

Our take

Kubota petrol front-mount vs diesel front-mount. The F251 has the Briggs Vanguard petrol; the F3090 has the Kubota D1503 diesel. £9000 gap. The F251 is the entry for occasional out-front use, F3090 for commercial daily routes.

Pros & cons of each

Kubota F251

Pros

  • Cheapest way into a Kubota out-front mower
  • 4WD across all four wheels for proper banks and slopes
  • Vanguard V-twin, agricultural-grade petrol engine
  • Out-front deck cuts right up to walls and trees

Cons

  • Petrol, not diesel, in a class where diesel is the norm
  • Side-discharge or mulch only, no collection box
  • Mowing speed lower than a comparable Z-series zero-turn
Kubota F3090

Pros

  • D1503 diesel, the workhorse engine, 8000-hour service life proven
  • Permanent 4WD with proper front diff
  • Optional Kubota PowerFold collector for proper estate spec
  • Out-front deck plus diesel torque equals serious productivity

Cons

  • Proper commercial money new
  • Heavy at 780kg, marks soft turf
  • Diesel particulate filter on later models adds maintenance complexity
