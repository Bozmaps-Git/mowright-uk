Buying Guide

Practical advice for buying a mower secondhand or new in the UK.

Facebook Marketplace Tips

Sellers routinely overprice by 20–30%. Lead with the Used Avg figure on the listing — if they want more, ask why. Always insist on seeing it run before cash changes hands. Pay on collection, not before.

Petrol Check List

Cold-start it: a hot engine hides starting issues. Pull the dipstick — black or milky oil is bad news. Test the self-propel on a slope, not a flat driveway. Lift it, look at the blade and deck for cracks or weld repairs.

Cordless Battery Age

Lithium cells degrade after roughly 400 charge cycles. Ask how old the battery is, not the mower. Run it for 15 minutes — short runtime means dead cells, and a replacement battery is often half the price of a new mower.

Robot Mower Traps

Boundary wire breaks are the #1 fault — walk the perimeter. Make sure the seller transfers the app account or factory-resets it. GPS antenna and base station must be included. Demand a live cut demo before paying.

Ride-On Red Flags

Engine hours matter more than years. Test the PTO clutch — it should engage cleanly with no slip. Steering pulling left or right is a tracking or front-axle fault. No service history means assume the worst and price accordingly.

Brand Resale Value

Honda, Husqvarna, Stihl and EGO hold value best — expect 50–60% of new after 3 years. Budget brands (Webb, Hyundai, Einhell) lose value fast and rarely recover even at low prices. Buy quality used or budget new — never the reverse.
