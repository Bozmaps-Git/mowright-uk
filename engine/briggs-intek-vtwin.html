The story of this engine

The Briggs Intek 7180 V-twin is the engine inside the Murray MX175-107, the Cub Cadet XT2, and many John Deere E-series tractors. Briggs sells it to anyone who wants it, so the same block sits under wildly different deck colours and price tags. Smooth, durable, and well-supported — the obvious upgrade from a single-cylinder Intek for any half-acre+ lawn.

Why owners like it

  • Smoother running than any single-cylinder ride-on engine
  • Same parts work across Murray, John Deere, Cub Cadet — wide aftermarket support
  • 1,500-hour service life is realistic for domestic owners (25+ years of weekend use)
  • Self-cleaning rotating air screen reduces filter changes

Where it falls short

  • Splash lubrication (not pressurised) — needs oil checked every 5 hours
  • Flywheel key shears if the deck ingests a rock — symptom: cranks but no spark
  • Pricier service parts than a single-cylinder Intek

What goes wrong, and when

Every engine family has predictable failures by age. Knowing what's coming saves money on used buys and makes negotiation easier.

Hours 1–500
None expected. Intek V-twins are notably trouble-free in early life.
Hours 500–1,000
Rocker arm wear shows up as valve clatter at idle. Adjust valves; if still noisy, replace rockers (£25 each).
Hours 1,000–1,500
Carb diaphragm hardens — engine surges or stumbles under load. £18 service kit.
Hours 1,500+
Top-end rebuild may be needed (head gaskets, valve seats). £140 parts, full-day job — or £400 short-block from Briggs.
Any age
Sheared flywheel key after deck impact. Symptom: cranks but no spark. £8 part, 1-hour DIY.

The verdict

The Intek V-twin is the engine you should look for on a used ride-on. Skip Intek single-cylinder if you can stretch the budget — the V-twin will outlast it by 50% and runs noticeably smoother.
